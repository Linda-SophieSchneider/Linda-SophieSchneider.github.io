I am a PhD student at the Pattern Recognition Lab at FAU Erlangen-Nürnberg in Germany, under the supervision of Andreas Maier.

Research

I am focused on solving challenges in industrial computed tomography (CT), particularly in optimizing CT trajectories and improving reconstruction. My research explores how AI tools can enhance image quality and reduce scanning times. Collaborating with the Fraunhofer EZRT in Fürth, I work primarily on robotic CT systems and industry CT machines, aiming to combine practical solutions with innovative technology.

Education

Currently, I am pursuing my PhD under the supervision of Andreas Maier since 2021 at Friedrich-Alexander-University Erlangen-Nürnberg, Germany. Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Mathematics from Friedrich-Alexander-University Erlangen-Nürnberg between 2018 and 2021. Earlier, I did my Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Mathematics and Economics at Friedrich-Alexander-University Erlangen-Nürnberg from 2014 to 2018.
